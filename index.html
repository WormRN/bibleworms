The Bible is for you.

You do not have to be a Bible expert to begin. BibleWorms gives you a gentle path for reading, noticing, understanding, and responding.

Slow down.

Many people read quickly, feel confused, and quit. Bible study often begins by reading less but noticing more.

Listen first.

Before we ask, “What does this mean to me?” we ask, “What is God saying in this passage?”

Respond faithfully.

The goal is not to finish a worksheet. The goal is changed belief and changed life before God.

Two paths. One purpose.

GOAL is simple. EDGE is thorough. Both are transformational.

The difference is not shallow versus deep. GOAL can go very deep when it leads to real faith and obedience. EDGE is more thorough because it pays closer attention to words, context, culture, literary form, structure, and meaning.

Primary path

GOAL

The simple study method. Use GOAL first. It is direct, memorable, and built for daily reading, groups, families, and new believers.

  • Best for regular Bible reading
  • Moves quickly toward faithful response
  • Simple enough to repeat often

GOAL

Set a GOAL. This is the primary BibleWorms method: four simple questions that help you see God, understand people, align with truth, and live it out.

The simple study method

Start with GOAL.

GOAL is not shallow. It is simple and direct. If it helps you trust God, repent honestly, forgive someone, serve a neighbor, pray with faith, or change direction, it has done serious spiritual work.

G

God

What does this teach about God?

Start with God before you start with yourself. What does this passage show about his character, promises, commands, warnings, mercy, holiness, power, love, or purpose? How does it point toward Jesus?

O

Others

What does this teach about others?

Scripture tells the truth about human beings. What does this show about our desires, fears, sins, weakness, faith, foolishness, wisdom, need, or hope?

A

Align

Where do I need to align with this truth?

What belief needs to change? What lie have I been believing? What desire, habit, attitude, or way of thinking needs to be brought into line with God’s Word?

L

Live it out

How will I live out this truth?

Make it concrete. What will you do today or this week? Who needs to be loved, forgiven, served, encouraged, or contacted? What prayer should you pray?

The thorough study method

Use EDGE when the passage needs closer attention.

EDGE helps you slow down to observe, investigate, gather meaning, and respond. It is more thorough than GOAL, but it has the same purpose: changed belief and changed life.

A loving warning

EDGE is a helpful tool, but more thorough study does not automatically mean more faithful living. It is possible to keep learning while avoiding the truth God has already made clear.

Do not use EDGE to delay obedience. If God’s Word has already shown you the next faithful step, take it. The goal is not to master a method. The goal is to be formed by the Word of God.

E — Explore

Uses READ, with WORD nested under Define
R — Read

Read the section at least twice.

E — Examine

Examine the passage in another translation or form.

A — Analyze

Stew in it. Meditate. Think carefully before rushing ahead.

D — Define

Define difficult or important words.

WORD under Define:

W Word — choose the English word. O Original — find the original-language word. R Range — consider the range of meanings. D Decide — choose the meaning in this context.

Uses DIG
D — Details

Ask who, what, when, where, why, and how.

I — Investigate

Learn about context, characters, culture, and composition.

G — Grasp
